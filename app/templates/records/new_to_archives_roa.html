The catalogue

The catalogue is a digital index of records. Each record is represented by a description page that provides information about it. Whether you are a member of the public, a researcher, or a student, you can use the catalogue to search and find records you might be interested in. You can filter for records held at other archives or for those held at The National Archives.

Records at other archives

It's our role to support the UK archive sector and help secure the future of physical and digital records. We invite archives of all kinds to list and manage their collections on our catalogue. This offers a way to get collections data online, widen access, and create a single place to explore a vast range of archive collections relating to British history.

Current archives in the catalogue include local authority archives, community archives, university archives, historic houses, business archives, specialist repositories, libraries and museums.

How to see records at other archives

The catalogue shows descriptions of records, not the record itself. To view a record held by another archive, you'll need to visit the website of the archive that holds it. Contact details of the archive can be found on Find an archive. Some records may be available to access online, while others can only be viewed in person.
